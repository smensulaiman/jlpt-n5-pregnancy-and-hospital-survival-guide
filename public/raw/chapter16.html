Chapter Sixteen

Checkout

退院 — Taiin

16.1 Situation 場面

Discharge day (退院, taiin). You settle the bill, use the childbirth lump-sum grant, book the one-month checkup, and take your family home. The key financial term: 出産育児一時金 (shussan ikuji ichijikin) — a government lump-sum grant (currently ¥500,000) that is usually paid directly to the hospital, so you only pay the difference.

16.8 Cultural Notes 文化ノート

Money & Paperwork at Discharge

  • 出産育児一時金 — the ¥500,000 grant. Under the "direct payment system" (直接支払制度), the health insurer pays the hospital directly; you pay only the excess. Sign up for this during pregnancy at the hospital, or you'll have to front the whole amount and claim it back.
  • Normal birth is not covered by insurance. Childbirth is not an "illness," so the 30% insurance rule does not apply — the grant is the mechanism instead. A C-section is covered by insurance, and may also trigger 高額療養費 (high-cost medical expense reimbursement).
  • Keep every receipt. Medical expenses over ¥100,000 in a year can be deducted from tax (医療費控除). Birth costs usually qualify. Keep receipts including taxi fares to the hospital on the day of labor.
  • Some hospitals are cash only. Confirm in advance: 「カードは使えますか。」 If not, you'll need to bring ¥100,000+ in cash on discharge day.
  • The 14-day clock. 出生届 (birth registration) must be filed at the city office within 14 days. Bring: the 出生証明書 from the hospital, the 母子手帳, your 印鑑, and your residence card. Do this together with the health insurance registration and the child allowance (児童手当) application — one trip, three forms.
  • Child car seat is legally required in Japan for children under 6. Hospitals will ask. Have it installed before discharge day.
