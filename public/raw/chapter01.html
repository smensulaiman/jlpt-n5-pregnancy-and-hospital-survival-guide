Chapter One

Emergency Preparation

緊急の準備 — Kinkyū no junbi

1.1 Situation 場面

It is 2 a.m. Your wife wakes you up: the contractions have started, or her water has broken. In the next thirty minutes you may need to call the clinic, call a taxi — or, if something is wrong, call 119 for an ambulance. This chapter gives you every sentence you need for that half hour, in the order you will actually use them.

Two key words first: 陣痛 (jintsū) means "labor pains / contractions," and 破水 (hasui) means "water breaking." Both are above N5 level, but they are the two most important words in this entire book. Hospital staff will use them constantly.

1.3 Conversation 1 — Labor Has Started 陣痛が始まった

2:00 a.m. Your wife wakes you.

SpeakerJapaneseRomajiEnglish
Wifeねえ、起きて。お腹が痛い。Nee, okite. Onaka ga itai.Hey, wake up. My stomach hurts.
Husband大丈夫？陣痛？Daijōbu? Jintsū?Are you okay? Contractions?
Wifeうん、たぶん陣痛が始まった。Un, tabun jintsū ga hajimatta.Yes, I think labor has started.
Husband分かった。病院に電話するね。Wakatta. Byōin ni denwa suru ne.Got it. I'll call the hospital.

1.4 Conversation 2 — Her Water Broke 破水した

A different possible beginning: the water breaks first.

SpeakerJapaneseRomajiEnglish
Wifeあ、破水したかもしれない。A, hasui shita kamo shirenai.Oh — I think my water may have broken.
Husband本当？痛みはある？Hontō? Itami wa aru?Really? Do you have any pain?
Wife痛みはまだない。でも、水が出ている。Itami wa mada nai. Demo, mizu ga dete iru.No pain yet. But water is coming out.
Husband分かった。タオルを持ってくる。座って待っていて。Wakatta. Taoru o motte kuru. Suwatte matte ite.Okay. I'll bring a towel. Sit down and wait.
Husband今から病院に電話するよ。Ima kara byōin ni denwa suru yo.I'm calling the hospital now.
Wifeうん、お願い。母子手帳もお願いね。Un, onegai. Boshi techō mo onegai ne.Yes, please. And please get the Mother-Child Handbook.

Important

If the water breaks, most Japanese hospitals want the mother to come in immediately, even without contractions, and usually by car or taxi — not walking. When you phone, say clearly: 破水しました (Hasui shimashita — "Her water broke").

1.5 Conversation 3 — Calling the Clinic 病院に電話

Maternity hospitals have a 24-hour phone line. A nurse or midwife answers.

SpeakerJapaneseRomajiEnglish
Nurseはい、さくら産婦人科です。Hai, Sakura sanfujinka desu.Hello, Sakura OB-GYN Clinic.
Husband夜分にすみません。田中の夫です。妻の陣痛が始まりました。Yabun ni sumimasen. Tanaka no otto desu. Tsuma no jintsū ga hajimarimashita.Sorry to call so late. I'm Mrs. Tanaka's husband. My wife's labor has started.
Nurse田中さんですね。陣痛は何分おきですか。Tanaka-san desu ne. Jintsū wa nanpun oki desu ka.Mrs. Tanaka, yes. How many minutes apart are the contractions?
Husband十分おきぐらいです。Juppun oki gurai desu.About every ten minutes.
Nurse破水はありましたか。Hasui wa arimashita ka.Has her water broken?
Husbandいいえ、まだです。Iie, mada desu.No, not yet.
Nurse分かりました。では、病院に来てください。気をつけて来てくださいね。Wakarimashita. Dewa, byōin ni kite kudasai. Ki o tsukete kite kudasai ne.Understood. Please come to the hospital. Come carefully.
Husbandはい、三十分ぐらいで着きます。よろしくお願いします。Hai, sanjuppun gurai de tsukimasu. Yoroshiku onegai shimasu.Yes, we'll arrive in about thirty minutes. Thank you very much.

Operatorかしこまりました。十分ほどで着きます。Kashikomarimashita. Juppun hodo de tsukimasu.Certainly. The taxi will arrive in about ten minutes.
Husbandありがとうございます。急いでお願いします。Arigatō gozaimasu. Isoide onegai shimasu.Thank you. Please hurry.

Jinpu Taxi · 陣痛タクシー

Many Japanese cities have a 陣痛タクシー (jintsū takushī) — a "labor taxi" service. You register your address and hospital in advance; when you call, drivers already know where to go and carry waterproof seats. Register during pregnancy — it is free and removes all language stress on the big night.

1.7 Conversation 5 — Calling an Ambulance (119) 救急車を呼ぶ

Use 119 only for a true emergency: heavy bleeding, unbearable pain, the baby coming very fast, or the mother unable to move.

Operatorはい、一一九番です。火事ですか、救急ですか。Hai, hyakujūkyū-ban desu. Kaji desu ka, kyūkyū desu ka.This is 119. Fire or medical emergency?
Husband救急です。妻が妊娠しています。出血がひどいです。Kyūkyū desu. Tsuma ga ninshin shite imasu. Shukketsu ga hidoi desu.Medical emergency. My wife is pregnant. She is bleeding heavily.
Operator分かりました。住所を教えてください。Wakarimashita. Jūsho o oshiete kudasai.Understood. Please tell me your address.

Operator奥さんは今、意識がありますか。Okusan wa ima, ishiki ga arimasu ka.Is your wife conscious right now?
Husbandはい、あります。でも、とても痛がっています。妊娠三十八週です。Hai, arimasu. Demo, totemo itagatte imasu. Ninshin sanjūhasshū desu.Yes, she is. But she is in a lot of pain. She is 38 weeks pregnant.
Operator分かりました。すぐ救急車が行きます。かかりつけの病院はありますか。Wakarimashita. Sugu kyūkyūsha ga ikimasu. Kakaritsuke no byōin wa arimasu ka.Understood. An ambulance is on its way. Does she have a regular hospital?

1.10 Cultural Notes 文化ノート

Things Foreign Residents Should Know

  • Call before you go. In Japan you never simply show up at a maternity hospital at night. Always phone the 24-hour line first; the midwife decides when you should come.
  • 119, not 110. 119 is fire and ambulance; 110 is police. Ambulances in Japan are free of charge, but they are for genuine emergencies only — a normal labor is usually a taxi trip, not an ambulance trip.
  • The 母子手帳 is sacred. The Maternal and Child Health Handbook contains the whole pregnancy record. Hospital staff will ask for it before almost anything else. Keep it, the insurance card, and the hospital card together in one pouch by the door from week 34.
  • Pack the bag by week 35. Japanese hospitals give a printed list (pajamas, towels, pads, cash). Many payments still require cash or a specific machine, so keep ¥20,000–30,000 in the bag.
  • Speak slowly, not loudly. If you are not understood, repeat the key noun: 「陣痛です」「破水です」. Single clear keywords work better than long sentences.
