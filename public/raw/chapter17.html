Chapter Seventeen

One-Month Checkup

一か月健診 — Ikkagetsu kenshin

17.1 Situation 場面

The 一か月健診 is the first big milestone after going home. Both mother and baby are examined: the baby's weight, height, head circumference, reflexes; the mother's uterus, stitches, and mental health. Bring the 母子手帳 — the doctor writes the results in it.

17.8 Cultural Notes 文化ノート

Postnatal Support in Japan

  • 保健センター (hoken sentā) — the public health center. Every municipality has one. A 保健師 (public health nurse) will visit your home free of charge in the first months to weigh the baby and check on the mother. Say yes to this visit — it is genuinely useful and it is your right as a resident.
  • 産後うつ (postnatal depression) is taken seriously now. The one-month checkup includes a mood questionnaire. Encourage your wife to answer honestly, not politely. Foreign mothers in Japan are at higher risk because of isolation and language.
  • Vaccination schedules are dense. Japanese babies receive many vaccines starting at 2 months, often 4–6 injections in a single visit (同時接種). This is normal and considered safe here.
  • The growth curve (成長曲線). The 母子手帳 has printed growth graphs. The doctor plots your baby on them at every checkup. Ask to see the page — it's a great way to understand the explanation even with limited Japanese.
  • Next checkups: 3–4 months, 6–7 months, 9–10 months, 1 year, 1.5 years, 3 years. Most are free at the city's designated clinics.
