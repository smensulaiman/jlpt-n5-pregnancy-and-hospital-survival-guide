Chapter Twelve

Breastfeeding

授乳 — Junyū

12.1 Situation 場面

Feeding (授乳, junyū) is taught by the midwife in the first days. She will show holding positions, latching, burping, and how to combine breast milk (母乳, bonyū) with formula (ミルク, miruku). Japanese hospitals name the holds precisely — you will hear 横抱き (cradle), 縦抱き (upright), 交差抱き (cross-cradle), 脇抱き (football hold).

Two words that confuse everyone

母乳 (bonyū) = breast milk. ミルク (miruku) = infant formula — not ordinary milk. If a nurse asks 「ミルクにしますか」 she is asking whether to give formula, not cow's milk.

12.6 Longer Dialogue — Milk Isn't Enough 母乳が足りない

Day three. The baby cries constantly; the mother is worried and exhausted.

SpeakerJapaneseRomajiEnglish
Wife赤ちゃんがずっと泣いています。母乳が足りないんでしょうか。Akachan ga zutto naite imasu. Bonyū ga tarinai n deshō ka.The baby keeps crying. Is my milk not enough?
Midwife大丈夫ですよ。三日目は、まだ母乳が少ないのが普通です。Daijōbu desu yo. Mikkame wa, mada bonyū ga sukunai no ga futsū desu.It's all right. On day three, it's normal for milk to still be low.
Wifeでも、赤ちゃんがおなかをすかせています。かわいそうです。Demo, akachan ga onaka o sukasete imasu. Kawaisō desu.But the baby is hungry. I feel sorry for her.
Midwifeでは、ミルクを足しましょう。母乳のあとに、ミルクを四十ミリあげてください。Dewa, miruku o tashimashō. Bonyū no ato ni, miruku o yonjū miri agete kudasai.Then let's supplement with formula. After breastfeeding, give 40 ml of formula.
Husbandミルクの作り方を教えてください。Miruku no tsukurikata o oshiete kudasai.Please teach me how to make the formula.
Midwifeはい。粉ミルクをスプーン二杯、お湯を四十ミリ入れます。よく振ってください。Hai. Kona miruku o supūn nihai, oyu o yonjū miri iremasu. Yoku futte kudasai.Yes. Two scoops of powder, 40 ml of hot water. Shake well.
Husband熱くないですか。Atsukunai desu ka.Isn't it too hot?
Midwife冷ましてください。手首に一滴たらして、熱くなければ大丈夫です。Samashite kudasai. Tekubi ni itteki tarashite, atsukunakereba daijōbu desu.Let it cool. Drop one drop on your wrist; if it's not hot, it's fine.
Husband分かりました。僕もミルクをあげられますね。Wakarimashita. Boku mo miruku o ageraremasu ne.Understood. So I can feed her too.
Midwifeはい、ぜひ。お父さんが夜のミルクをあげると、お母さんが休めますよ。Hai, zehi. Otōsan ga yoru no miruku o ageru to, okāsan ga yasumemasu yo.Yes, please do. If Father gives the night feed, Mother can rest.
Wifeありがとう。少し安心しました。おっぱいがはって、痛いです。Arigatō. Sukoshi anshin shimashita. Oppai ga hatte, itai desu.Thank you. I feel a bit better. My breasts are engorged and painful.
Midwife冷やしましょう。冷たいタオルを当ててください。あとでマッサージもします。Hiyashimashō. Tsumetai taoru o atete kudasai. Ato de massāji mo shimasu.Let's cool them. Apply a cold towel. I'll also massage them later.
Wifeお願いします。助かります。Onegai shimasu. Tasukarimasu.Please. That's a real help.

12.8 Cultural Notes 文化ノート

Feeding in Japan

  • 母乳 is strongly encouraged. Japanese hospitals push breastfeeding hard, and some mothers feel pressure and guilt if milk is slow. If your wife is struggling, say clearly: 「母乳が足りないので、ミルクを足したいです。」 Formula is not failure.
  • 授乳室 (junyūshitsu). Nursing rooms exist in almost every Japanese department store, station, and mall — usually clean, free, with hot water for formula. Look for the sign 授乳室 or ベビールーム.
  • Fathers can do everything except breastfeed. Taking the night formula feed is the single most valuable thing you can do this week. Ask the midwife to teach you: 「ミルクの作り方を教えてください。」
  • Breast massage (母乳マッサージ) is a real service. Japanese midwives are trained in it and it is standard postpartum care. Encourage your wife to accept it — it prevents 乳腺炎 (nyūsen'en, mastitis).
  • Watch for mastitis. If a breast becomes hot, hard, red, and she has a fever, tell the nurse immediately: 「おっぱいが熱くて、熱があります。」 This needs treatment.
