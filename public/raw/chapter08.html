Chapter Eight

Labor Room

陣痛室 — Jintsūshitsu

8.1 Situation 場面

This is the long part — often 6 to 12 hours in the 陣痛室 (jintsūshitsu, labor room). Contractions get stronger and closer together. Your job as the husband is simple and enormous: be there, breathe with her, rub her back, and encourage her. This chapter is mostly encouragement (はげまし, hagemashi) — the sentences you will say a hundred times tonight.

8.9 Cultural Notes 文化ノート

Being a Husband in a Japanese Labor Room

  • Presence is the job. Japanese midwives do not expect the husband to give medical opinions. They expect him to be calm, quiet, and physically helpful: water, ice, towel, back massage, hand to hold.
  • Don't leave without saying so. If you step out, tell the midwife: 「ちょっと出ます。すぐ戻ります。」 ("I'm stepping out. I'll be right back.") Staff need to know who is in the room.
  • The 促進剤 conversation. If labor stalls, the doctor may suggest a 陣痛促進剤 (jintsū sokushinzai, labor-inducing drug) by IV drip. This requires a signed consent form. Ask: 「なぜ必要ですか。」 ("Why is it necessary?")
  • Eat and drink — you too. Labor can last 20 hours. Hospitals have vending machines and a convenience store. A husband who faints is not helping. Keep a rice ball and water in your bag.
  • Silence between contractions is normal. Don't fill every second with talk. Let her rest in the gaps; be ready when the next one comes.
