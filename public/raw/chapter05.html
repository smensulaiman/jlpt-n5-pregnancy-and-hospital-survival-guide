Chapter Five

Going to the Room

病室へ — Byōshitsu e

5.1 Situation 場面

Paperwork is done. A nurse takes you up to the room — either a private room (個室) or a shared room (大部屋). Your wife will change into a hospital gown (着がえる, kigaeru) and you'll unpack. You need to know your room number, where the toilet is, and where you — the husband — are allowed to be.

5.6 Longer Dialogue — Settling In 部屋の説明

The nurse explains the room: the nurse call, the bed, the rules, and whether the husband can stay.

SpeakerJapaneseRomajiEnglish
Nurseお部屋の説明をします。こちらがナースコールです。Oheya no setsumei o shimasu. Kochira ga nāsu kōru desu.Let me explain the room. This is the nurse call button.
Husbandこれを押せば、看護師さんが来ますか。Kore o oseba, kangoshi-san ga kimasu ka.If we press this, a nurse will come?
Nurseはい、すぐ来ます。痛みが強くなったら、遠慮なく押してください。Hai, sugu kimasu. Itami ga tsuyoku nattara, enryo naku oshite kudasai.Yes, we'll come right away. If the pain gets stronger, don't hesitate to press it.
Wife分かりました。ベッドで横になってもいいですか。Wakarimashita. Beddo de yoko ni natte mo ii desu ka.Understood. May I lie down on the bed?
Nurseはい、どうぞ。楽な姿勢で休んでください。Hai, dōzo. Raku na shisei de yasunde kudasai.Yes, please. Rest in a comfortable position.
Husbandあの、僕はここにいてもいいですか。Ano, boku wa koko ni ite mo ii desu ka.Um, may I stay here?
Nurseはい、個室ですから、付き添いは大丈夫です。ソファで休めますよ。Hai, koshitsu desu kara, tsukisoi wa daijōbu desu. Sofa de yasumemasu yo.Yes, since it's a private room, staying with her is fine. You can rest on the sofa.
Husbandよかったです。夜も泊まってもいいですか。Yokatta desu. Yoru mo tomatte mo ii desu ka.That's a relief. May I also stay overnight?
Nurseはい、大丈夫です。でも、大部屋の場合は面会時間だけです。Hai, daijōbu desu. Demo, ōbeya no baai wa menkai jikan dake desu.Yes, that's fine. But in a shared room, it's only during visiting hours.
Husband分かりました。面会時間は何時から何時までですか。Wakarimashita. Menkai jikan wa nanji kara nanji made desu ka.Understood. What are the visiting hours?
Nurse午後二時から八時までです。それから、廊下では静かにお願いします。Gogo niji kara hachiji made desu. Sorekara, rōka de wa shizuka ni onegai shimasu.From 2 p.m. to 8 p.m. Also, please be quiet in the corridor.
Husbandはい、気をつけます。ありがとうございます。Hai, ki o tsukemasu. Arigatō gozaimasu.Yes, I'll be careful. Thank you very much.

5.8 Cultural Notes 文化ノート

Room Etiquette in a Japanese Hospital

  • 遠慮 (enryo) is the enemy. Japanese politeness teaches you to hold back — but nurses will repeatedly say 「遠慮なく」 ("without hesitation"). They mean it. Press the nurse call when your wife needs help. It is not rude.
  • Shared rooms have curtains, not walls. In a 大部屋, everything you say is heard by three other families. Speak softly. Husbands generally cannot sleep in a shared room — only in a 個室.
  • Slippers indoors. Patients wear slippers or soft shoes in the room. Don't walk in outdoor shoes across a hospital room floor if slippers are provided.
  • Phones: Set your phone to silent (マナーモード, "manner mode"). Calls in the room or corridor are often prohibited; use the designated phone corner.
  • Valuables: Hospitals accept no responsibility for cash left in rooms. Use the locker (usually with a coin-return key) and keep only what you need.
