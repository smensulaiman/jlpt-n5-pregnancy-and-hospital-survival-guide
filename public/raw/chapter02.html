Chapter Two

Arriving at the Hospital

病院に到着 — Byōin ni tōchaku

2.1 Situation 場面

The taxi pulls up. It is the middle of the night, the main entrance is dark, and you need to find the night entrance or emergency entrance (夜間入口 / 救急入口). You may need to speak to the taxi driver, a security guard, and the night receptionist — possibly while carrying two bags and supporting your wife. This chapter covers everything between the car door and the reception desk.

2.9 Cultural Notes 文化ノート

Hospital Etiquette on Arrival

  • Shoes: Many smaller maternity clinics ask you to change into slippers at the entrance. Look for a shoe shelf (下駄箱, getabako) with rows of slippers. Big general hospitals usually let you keep shoes on.
  • Masks: Hospitals commonly expect masks indoors. Keep two masks in the hospital bag. If you forget, reception often sells them for ¥50–100.
  • Quiet at night: Speak in a low voice in corridors — other mothers and newborns are sleeping. Phone calls are only allowed in designated areas (携帯電話コーナー).
  • The intercom ritual: At night, everything goes through the intercom (インターホン). State your family name and one keyword: 「田中です。陣痛です。」 That is enough — staff will take over.
  • Taxi payment: Pay quickly with IC card or exact cash if possible. It is normal and not rude to leave immediately; Japanese taxi doors open automatically — don't slam them.
