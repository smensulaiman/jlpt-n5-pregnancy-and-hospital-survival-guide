Chapter Nine

Delivery Room

分娩室 — Bunbenshitsu

9.1 Situation 場面

The cervix is fully open (全開). Your wife is moved to the delivery room (分娩室) and onto the delivery table (分娩台). From here the instructions come fast and loud. You do not need to understand every word — you need to recognize six commands, listed below. Learn these six and you can follow the entire birth.

9.9 Cultural Notes 文化ノート

The Japanese Delivery Room

  • Cutting the cord is often offered. Many Japanese hospitals invite the father to cut the umbilical cord (へその緒). If you want to, say so beforehand: 「へその緒を切りたいです。」
  • The birth cry matters. The 産声 (ubugoe, first cry) is emotionally significant in Japan. Staff will comment on how strong it is — this is their way of telling you the baby is breathing well.
  • Skin-to-skin (カンガルーケア). Most hospitals place the baby on the mother's chest immediately. It is called カンガルーケア or 早期母子接触.
  • Photos: Ask before filming. Many hospitals allow photos after the birth but not during. 「写真を撮ってもいいですか。」
  • The placenta (胎盤) comes after. Do not be alarmed when the staff keep working after the baby is out — delivering the placenta and stitching any tear (会陰切開, ein sekkai, episiotomy) takes another 20–30 minutes. This is normal.
  • Thank everyone. When it's over, say to the whole team: 「ありがとうございました。お世話になりました。」 It will be remembered.
