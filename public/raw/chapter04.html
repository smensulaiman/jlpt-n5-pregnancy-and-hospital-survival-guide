Chapter Four

Admission

入院手続き — Nyūin tetsuzuki

4.1 Situation 場面

Admission (入院, nyūin) means signing forms and answering medical-history questions. The staff will ask about allergies, previous pregnancies, the due date, and past illnesses. These questions come from the 問診票 (monshinhyō, "medical questionnaire") — the same form printed in your 母子手帳. This chapter gives you the words to answer clearly even when the questions come fast.

4.8 Cultural Notes 文化ノート

Paperwork & Privacy in Japan

  • The husband can sign. When the mother is in pain, the husband (as guarantor / 保証人, hoshōnin) usually signs admission and consent forms. Bring your own 在留カード and 印鑑 (inkan, personal seal) if you have one — some forms ask for a seal instead of a signature.
  • Consent forms are real. The 同意書 for delivery, anesthesia, or a possible C-section (帝王切開, teiō sekkai) is legally meaningful. If you don't understand, say 「意味が分かりません。説明してください。」 ("I don't understand the meaning. Please explain.") — never sign blindly.
  • State allergies loudly and early. Repeat drug allergies twice; write them in English capitals on the form too. Staff take 薬アレルギー (drug allergy) extremely seriously.
  • Privacy is protected. Japanese hospitals will not share the mother's condition with anyone not listed as family or emergency contact. Make sure your name is on the 緊急連絡先 line.
  • Keep every receipt. Admission generates paperwork you'll need later for the lump-sum childbirth grant (出産育児一時金) and insurance. Put all papers in one folder from day one.
