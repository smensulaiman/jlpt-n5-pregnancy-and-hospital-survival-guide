Chapter Six

Meeting the Nurse

看護師との会話 — Kangoshi to no kaiwa

6.1 Situation 場面

The nurse (看護師, kangoshi) now checks your wife's condition: pain level, contraction timing, blood pressure (血圧測定), temperature, and the baby's movements. She will also coach the breathing. Two phrases you will hear over and over in this hospital and in the delivery room: 深呼吸してください (shinkokyū shite kudasai — "take a deep breath") and 力をぬいてください (chikara o nuite kudasai — "relax / let the strength go").

6.8 Cultural Notes 文化ノート

Nurses, Midwives, and Pain in Japan

  • The midwife runs the birth. In Japan the 助産師 (josanshi, midwife) — not the doctor — is usually with the mother the whole time. The doctor appears for examinations and the delivery itself. Direct your everyday questions to the midwife.
  • Natural birth is the default. Epidurals (無痛分娩, mutsū bunben) exist but are far less common than in the West, cost extra, and must usually be arranged months in advance. If you want one, ask at the very first prenatal visit, not on the night.
  • 「がんばって」 is the core word. Nurses will say がんばってください ("do your best / hold on") constantly. It is genuine encouragement, not pressure.
  • Massage is welcome. Husbands are actively expected to rub the lower back (腰をさする) and press the hips during contractions. Ask the midwife to show you the spot — she will.
  • Say when it hurts. Foreign patients often under-report pain out of politeness. Japanese staff cannot help what they don't hear. Teach your wife: 「痛いです」「がまんできません」 ("It hurts" / "I can't bear it").
