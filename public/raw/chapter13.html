Chapter Thirteen

Baby Care

赤ちゃんのお世話 — Akachan no osewa

13.1 Situation 場面

Before discharge, the hospital teaches you baby care: diaper changes, the baby bath (沐浴, mokuyoku), umbilical cord care, sleeping, and what to do about fever and medicine. Japanese hospitals run a formal 沐浴指導 (mokuyoku shidō, bathing class) — fathers are welcome and should attend.

13.3 Conversation 1 — Changing a Diaper おむつを替える

SpeakerJapaneseRomajiEnglish
Nurseおむつを替えてみましょう。やってみますか。Omutsu o kaete mimashō. Yatte mimasu ka.Let's try changing a diaper. Would you like to try?
Husbandはい、やってみます。教えてください。Hai, yatte mimasu. Oshiete kudasai.Yes, I'll try. Please show me.
Nurse足を持ち上げて、おしりふきできれいに拭いてください。前から後ろへ。Ashi o mochiagete, oshirifuki de kirei ni fuite kudasai. Mae kara ushiro e.Lift the legs and wipe clean with baby wipes. From front to back.
Husband前から後ろへ、ですね。Mae kara ushiro e, desu ne.From front to back, right?
Nurseはい、特に女の子は大切です。新しいおむつを下に入れて、テープを留めます。Hai, toku ni onna no ko wa taisetsu desu. Atarashii omutsu o shita ni irete, tēpu o tomemasu.Yes, especially for girls it's important. Put the new diaper underneath and fasten the tapes.
Husbandきつくないですか。Kitsukunai desu ka.Isn't it too tight?
Nurse指が二本入るぐらいがちょうどいいです。上手ですよ。Yubi ga nihon hairu gurai ga chōdo ii desu. Jōzu desu yo.Room for two fingers is just right. You're doing well.

13.4 Conversation 2 — The Umbilical Cord へその緒のケア

SpeakerJapaneseRomajiEnglish
Husbandへその緒はどうすればいいですか。Heso no o wa dō sureba ii desu ka.What should we do about the umbilical cord?
Nurse毎日、消毒してください。この綿棒とアルコールを使います。Mainichi, shōdoku shite kudasai. Kono menbō to arukōru o tsukaimasu.Disinfect it every day. Use this cotton swab and alcohol.
Husband痛くないですか。Itakunai desu ka.Doesn't it hurt the baby?
Nurse痛くありません。神経がないので大丈夫です。Itaku arimasen. Shinkei ga nai node daijōbu desu.It doesn't hurt. There are no nerves, so it's fine.
Husbandいつ取れますか。Itsu toremasu ka.When will it fall off?
Nurse一週間から二週間ぐらいです。自然に取れます。無理に取らないでください。Isshūkan kara nishūkan gurai desu. Shizen ni toremasu. Muri ni toranaide kudasai.About one to two weeks. It falls off naturally. Please don't pull it off.
Husband赤くなったら、どうしますか。Akaku nattara, dō shimasu ka.What if it turns red?
Nurse赤くなったり、においがしたら、病院に連絡してください。Akaku nattari, nioi ga shitara, byōin ni renraku shite kudasai.If it becomes red or smells, please contact the hospital.

13.5 Conversation 3 — Fever & Medicine 熱と薬

SpeakerJapaneseRomajiEnglish
Husband赤ちゃんが熱を出したら、どうしますか。Akachan ga netsu o dashitara, dō shimasu ka.What should we do if the baby gets a fever?
Nurse三十七度五分以上なら、すぐ病院に連絡してください。Sanjūnana-do gobu ijō nara, sugu byōin ni renraku shite kudasai.If it's 37.5 degrees or above, contact the hospital immediately.
Husband夜でもいいですか。Yoru demo ii desu ka.Even at night?
Nurseはい、夜でも大丈夫です。生まれたばかりの赤ちゃんの熱は危ないですから。Hai, yoru demo daijōbu desu. Umareta bakari no akachan no netsu wa abunai desu kara.Yes, night is fine. A fever in a newborn is dangerous.
Husband薬を飲ませてもいいですか。Kusuri o nomasete mo ii desu ka.May we give medicine?
Nurseいいえ。医師の指示なしに、薬を飲ませないでください。Iie. Ishi no shiji nashi ni, kusuri o nomasenaide kudasai.No. Do not give medicine without a doctor's instruction.
Husband分かりました。気をつけます。Wakarimashita. Ki o tsukemasu.Understood. We'll be careful.

13.6 Longer Dialogue — The Bathing Class 沐浴指導

A nurse teaches the father to bathe the baby — the famous 沐浴指導.

SpeakerJapaneseRomajiEnglish
Nurse今日は沐浴指導です。お父さん、やってみますか。Kyō wa mokuyoku shidō desu. Otōsan, yatte mimasu ka.Today is the bathing class. Father, would you like to try?
Husbandはい、やりたいです。でも、こわいです。落としそうで……。Hai, yaritai desu. Demo, kowai desu. Otoshisō de…….Yes, I want to. But I'm scared — I might drop her….
Nurse大丈夫です。ゆっくりやりましょう。まず、お湯の温度を確認します。Daijōbu desu. Yukkuri yarimashō. Mazu, oyu no ondo o kakunin shimasu.It's fine. Let's go slowly. First, check the water temperature.
Husband何度がいいですか。Nando ga ii desu ka.What temperature is good?
Nurse三十八度から四十度です。ひじで確認してもいいですよ。Sanjūhachi-do kara yonjū-do desu. Hiji de kakunin shite mo ii desu yo.38 to 40 degrees. You can also check with your elbow.
Husbandちょうどいいです。次は？Chōdo ii desu. Tsugi wa?It's just right. What's next?
Nurse赤ちゃんの頭と首を左手でしっかり支えます。ゆっくりお湯に入れてください。Akachan no atama to kubi o hidarite de shikkari sasaemasu. Yukkuri oyu ni irete kudasai.Support the head and neck firmly with your left hand. Lower her slowly into the water.
Husband（入れる）……あ、泣きません。気持ちよさそうです。(Ireru) …… A, nakimasen. Kimochi yosasō desu.(Lowering her in) …… Ah, she's not crying. She looks like she's enjoying it.
Nurse上手です。顔から洗います。ガーゼでやさしく拭いてください。Jōzu desu. Kao kara araimasu. Gāze de yasashiku fuite kudasai.Well done. Wash the face first. Wipe gently with gauze.
Husband石けんは使いますか。Sekken wa tsukaimasu ka.Do I use soap?
Nurseはい、ベビー石けんを使います。頭、体、最後におしりです。首やわきのしわもよく洗ってください。Hai, bebī sekken o tsukaimasu. Atama, karada, saigo ni oshiri desu. Kubi ya waki no shiwa mo yoku aratte kudasai.Yes, use baby soap. Head, body, then the bottom last. Wash the folds in the neck and armpits well too.
Husband時間はどれくらいですか。Jikan wa dore kurai desu ka.How long should it take?
Nurse五分から十分です。長すぎると疲れてしまいます。Gofun kara juppun desu. Nagasugiru to tsukarete shimaimasu.Five to ten minutes. Too long and she'll get tired.
Husband終わったら、どうしますか。Owattara, dō shimasu ka.What do I do when we're done?
Nurseすぐタオルで包んで、やさしく押さえるように拭いてください。こすらないでください。Sugu taoru de tsutsunde, yasashiku osaeru yō ni fuite kudasai. Kosuranaide kudasai.Wrap her in a towel right away and pat her dry gently. Don't rub.
Husbandできました！思ったより簡単でした。Dekimashita! Omotta yori kantan deshita.I did it! It was easier than I thought.
Nurseとても上手でした。家でも毎日、同じ時間にしてくださいね。Totemo jōzu deshita. Ie demo mainichi, onaji jikan ni shite kudasai ne.You did very well. At home, do it every day at the same time.

13.8 Cultural Notes 文化ノート

Baby Care the Japanese Way

  • 沐浴指導 is for fathers. Japanese hospitals expect fathers to attend the bathing class, and in many families bathing the baby becomes the father's permanent job. Volunteer for it.
  • Always on the back. Japanese guidance is strict: babies sleep あお向け (on their back), on a firm mattress, with no pillow and no soft toys. This is SIDS prevention (乳幼児突然死症候群).
  • 37.5°C is the magic number. For a newborn, 37.5 degrees or higher means call the hospital now — not tomorrow morning. Keep a digital thermometer at home.
  • Never medicate a newborn on your own. No adult medicine, no fever reducer, no herbal remedy without the doctor. This is stressed heavily in Japan.
  • The dried umbilical cord is kept. Japanese hospitals traditionally give the family a small wooden box (へその緒入れ) containing the dried cord stump. It's a keepsake, not a mistake — don't throw it away.
