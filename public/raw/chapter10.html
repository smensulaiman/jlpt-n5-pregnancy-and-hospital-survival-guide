Chapter Ten

After Delivery

出産のあと — Shussan no ato

10.1 Situation 場面

The baby is here. Now come the questions every new father asks in the first hour: Is my wife okay? Is the baby okay? Can I see the baby? Can I hold the baby? This chapter also covers photographs, calling family, and arranging visits.

10.8 Cultural Notes 文化ノート

The First Day — Japanese Customs

  • 母子同室 (boshi dōshitsu) — rooming-in. Most Japanese maternity hospitals keep mother and baby together from day one, so the mother learns feeding immediately. Some hospitals will take the baby at night if the mother is exhausted — you can ask: 「今夜だけ預かってもらえますか。」
  • Visiting is restricted. Japanese hospitals limit visitors strictly — often only the husband and grandparents, in narrow visiting hours, sometimes no children under 12. Check the rules before inviting anyone.
  • お疲れさまでした. This is the correct thing to say to your wife and to the staff. It means "you have worked hard" and is far more natural in Japanese than "congratulations" alone.
  • Wash your hands, every single time. Before touching the baby, always 手を洗う and use the alcohol dispenser. Staff notice this and it builds trust immediately.
  • The birth certificate clock starts now. You have 14 days to register the birth (出生届, shusshō todoke) at the city office. The hospital gives you a birth certificate form (出生証明書) signed by the doctor. Do not lose it. See Chapter 19.
