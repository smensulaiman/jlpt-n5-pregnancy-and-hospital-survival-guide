Chapter Fourteen

Doctor's Explanation

医師の説明 — Ishi no setsumei

14.1 Situation 場面

Before discharge, the doctor and the pediatrician (小児科の先生) explain the baby's health, the mother's recovery, medicines to take home, the vaccination schedule (予防接種, yobō sesshu), and the next appointments. Bring a notebook — and don't be afraid to ask for a slower repeat.

14.8 Cultural Notes 文化ノート

Understanding Japanese Medical Explanations

  • 異常なし (ijō nashi) = "no abnormality." This is the phrase you want to hear. It means everything is normal.
  • Vaccinations are free but not automatic. Routine vaccines (定期接種) are paid by your city. The city mails you a set of forms (予診票, yoshinhyō); you book the appointments yourself at a pediatric clinic. Nobody chases you — it is your responsibility.
  • The 母子手帳 is the vaccination record. Every shot gets stamped in the handbook. Schools and clinics will ask for it for years. Never lose it; photograph the pages as backup.
  • Ask for written notes. Japanese doctors are used to writing things down. 「紙に書いてもらえますか。」 is a completely normal request and solves most listening problems.
  • Ask about side effects. 副作用 (fukusayō) — pharmacists in Japan explain them thoroughly and give you a printed sheet with the medicine.
