Chapter Three

Reception

受付 — Uketsuke

3.1 Situation 場面

At the reception desk (受付, uketsuke) you present your documents and explain why you have come. In Japan you will always be asked for four key items: the hospital card (診察券), the health insurance card (保険証), the Maternal and Child Health Handbook (母子手帳), and — for foreign residents — often the residence card (在留カード). This chapter drills every sentence and every document.

3.8 Cultural Notes 文化ノート

Documents & Reception Etiquette

  • Hand documents with both hands. In Japan, cards and papers are passed and received with two hands, or with the right hand supported by the left. A small bow of the head as you hand over the 保険証 is normal and appreciated.
  • The four documents, memorized: 診察券 (hospital card) · 保険証 (insurance card) · 母子手帳 (handbook) · 在留カード (residence card). Keep them clipped together. Reception asks for them in almost this order.
  • 「失礼ですが」 is not rude. When staff say 失礼ですが ("excuse me, but…") before asking for your residence card, it is polite softening, not suspicion. All foreign residents are asked.
  • Private vs. shared room. A 個室 (private room) costs extra per night (差額ベッド代, sagaku beddo-dai) and is not covered by insurance. A 大部屋 (shared room, usually 4 beds) is free under insurance. Decide in advance.
  • Say your name humbly. Use 〜と申します (…to mōshimasu, "my name is…") rather than 〜です at first contact — it is the polite standard in hospitals and offices.
