Chapter Seven

Doctor Examination

医師の診察 — Ishi no shinsatsu

7.1 Situation 場面

The doctor comes to check how far labor has progressed. The key procedure is the 内診 (naishin, internal examination) — the doctor checks the cervix (子宮口, shikyūkō) and how many centimeters it has opened. You will also hear 超音波検査 / エコー (ultrasound) and 胎児心音 (the baby's heartbeat).

Words you must know before the doctor speaks

  • 内診 (naishin) — internal exam. The doctor will say something like 「下から診ますね」 ("I'll examine from below"). Underwear comes off; the wife lies on the exam table (診察台).
  • 子宮口 (shikyūkō) — the cervix. Measured in centimeters: 全開 (zenkai, fully open) = 10 cm.
  • エコー / 超音波検査 (ekō / chōonpa kensa) — ultrasound.
  • ノンストレステスト (non-sutoresu tesuto, NST) — a 40–60 minute monitor of contractions and the baby's heart rate, with two belts on the belly.

7.9 Cultural Notes 文化ノート

Talking with Japanese Doctors

  • Doctors are brief. Japanese doctors often give short explanations and don't expect many questions. This is not coldness — it's style. You are allowed to ask. Prepare 2–3 questions in advance and ask them clearly.
  • Ask for a slower repeat. The single most useful sentence in the hospital: 「すみません、もう一度ゆっくりお願いします。」
  • Interpreters exist. Larger hospitals have a 医療通訳 (iryō tsūyaku, medical interpreter) service, sometimes by tablet/video. Ask at the first prenatal visit: 「医療通訳はありますか。」
  • Attendance at birth (立ち会い, tachiai) is common but must be arranged in advance, and some hospitals require the husband to have attended a prenatal class. Confirm the policy months before.
  • Photos: Do not photograph or film during examinations without asking. In the delivery room, filming rules vary — ask first: 「写真を撮ってもいいですか。」
